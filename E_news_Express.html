Question : Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than the old landing page?¶

The selected test based on the given information is the 2-Sample independent t-test. Let's test whether the Z-test assumptions are satisfied or not:

  • Continuous Data - Yes, the time is measured on a continuous scale.
  • Normally distributed populations - Yes, all samples have more than 30 observations.
  • Independent populations - As we are taking random samples for two different groups from unique users, the samples are independent.
  • Unequal population standard deviations - Mean and Standard Deviation from sample are not the same(calculated below).
  • Random sampling from the population - Yes, we are informed that the collected sample a simple random sample.

We will test the following hyphotesis: time spent on the different pages (new x old) are equal or not

We will test the null hypothesis

$H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2$

against the alternate hypothesis

$H_a:\mu_1\neq\mu_2$

#Is this difference statistically significant?

from statsmodels.stats.proportion import proportions_ztest, proportion_confint
control_results = Enews[Enews['page'] == 'old']['converted']
treatment_results = Enews[Enews['page'] == 'new']['converted']
n_con = control_results.count()
n_treat = treatment_results.count()
successes = [control_results.sum(), treatment_results.sum()]
nobs = [n_con, n_treat]

z_stat, pval = proportions_ztest(successes, nobs=nobs, alternative = 'two-sided')
(lower_con, lower_treat), (upper_con, upper_treat) = proportion_confint(successes, nobs=nobs, alpha=0.05)

print(f'p-value: {pval:.3f}')
print(f'ci 95% for old landing page style: [{lower_con:.3f}, {upper_con:.3f}]')
print(f'ci 95% for new landing page style: [{lower_treat:.3f}, {upper_treat:.3f}]')

p-value: 0.070
ci 95% for old landing page style: [0.322, 0.598]
ci 95% for new landing page style: [0.507, 0.773]

Conclusion:¶

Since our p-value=0.07 is above our α=0.05 threshold, we cannot reject the Null hypothesis, which means that our new design did not perform better than our old one.
But, the p-value is very close to the level of significance, so let´s look additionally at the confidence interval for the new page :

([0.507, 0.773], or 50.7-77.3%) We notice that the lowest value is close to 50%, which means that there is still a posssibilty that the convertion rate is the same as the non convertion rate. Maybe a test with another sample, may give a more clear confirmation (or rejection).

Question: Is the mean time spent on the new page same for the different language users?¶

The selected test based on the given information is Anova Test

  • Anova test is used to determine wether the means of more than two independent populations are significantly different.
  • The populations are normally distributed - Yes, the normality assumption is verified using the Shapiro-Wilk’s test.
  • Samples are independent simple random samples - Yes, we are informed that the collected sample is a simple random sample.
  • Population variances are equal - Yes, the homogeneity of variance assumption is verified using the Levene's test. We will test the null hypothesis

$H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \mu_3$

against the alternative hypothesis

$H_a: $ At least one mean times are not equal
